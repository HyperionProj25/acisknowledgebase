**The governing rule for this entire page.** We do not claim that ACIS signals are caused by
fascial tissue changes. We present the fascial-science literature as a parallel evidence base
that is *consistent with* what ACIS observes, and we are designing research to test whether
they are the same signal at different levels. Every outbound word respects that boundary.

## Why this matters

SP12 detects fatigue patterns from pitch-level data. Separately, a decade of peer-reviewed
fascial science shows that connective tissue changes measurably under repeated loading, with
recovery running on more than one biological clock. The overlap between what ACIS detects and
what the tissue literature measures is striking, but overlap is not causation. This roadmap is
how we move from "consistent with" to "validated by."

## Proven vs hypothesis (the internal truth table)

**Proven:**

- ACIS detects fatigue patterns that track injured-list risk (this is the model's validated
  result).
- Posterior shoulder and lat stiffness rise measurably after throwing, and stiffness shifts
  appear before strength loss (multiple shear-wave elastography studies).
- The UCL softens measurably across a season.
- A meaningful share of force transmits laterally through fascial continuities.
- Connective-tissue recovery runs on at least two time constants (roughly 2-day gliding
  turnover, 3 to 7-plus-day structural).

**Hypothesis (not yet validated):**

- That ACIS's computational signals are actually detecting fascial tissue changes. No study
  has measured both in the same pitchers.
- That specific ACIS risk tiers correspond to specific tissue states.
- That modeling recovery as two clocks will improve the model's accuracy.

## The three horizons

**Horizon 1, reframe and document (now).** No model changes. Align the whole team on what is
proven, what is hypothesis, and where the line sits. Produce the internal driver-to-mechanism
reference and the positioning materials, all using "independently, the literature shows" and
"we are designing research to test."

**Horizon 2, build and test (3 to 6 months).** Fascial science starts informing features, each
one framed as a pre-registered experiment with a kill criterion. Candidates: dual time-constant
recovery features, stiffness-proxy features from pitch data, and (blocked on Hawk-Eye access)
posterior-chain load features. The test is simple: does the biologically motivated feature
improve accuracy by a pre-set margin? If yes, that is evidence. If no, we drop it and say so.

**Horizon 3, validate and publish (6 to 18 months).** The study nobody has run: measure ACIS
inputs and direct tissue state (shear-wave elastography) in the same 12 to 20 pitchers across a
season, with an academic partner for IRB and co-authorship. A positive result upgrades the
framework from "consistent with" to "validated by." A null result is published honestly, and
ACIS keeps running on its proven computational foundation.

## Dependencies and honesty

- The posterior-chain work is **blocked on MLB biomechanical (Hawk-Eye) data access.** There is
  no active LOI today; the current status is legal review. See [GTM Rails](/gtm-rails).
- Every horizon has pre-defined confirm and refute thresholds set before testing, not after.
- This is a living document. The rule that does not change: we go where the evidence takes us,
  report what we find, and never claim more than we have proven.
