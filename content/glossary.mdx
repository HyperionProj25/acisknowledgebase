Current ACIS vocabulary. The old reinforcement-learning terms live only in the
[Archive](/archive/glossary).

**ACIS.** Arm Care Intelligence System. A post-game arm-fatigue and workload-intelligence
system for MLB pitchers. Scores each appearance <Fact k="internal.scale" />.

**ACWR.** Acute-to-chronic workload ratio. Recent workload divided by longer-baseline workload.
Needs about four starts of baseline to compute, which sets the model's 5th-start floor.

**AUC.** Area under the ROC curve. A single number for how well the model separates injured from
healthy arms. 0.50 is random, 1.0 is perfect. SP12 scores <Fact k="validation.auc" /> on the blind cohort. Internal
term only; never used in outbound materials.

**Blind cohort.** The held-out validation set: <Fact k="validation.cohortSize" /> arms (<Fact k="validation.cases" /> injured, <Fact k="validation.controls" /> workload-matched
controls) the model never saw in training. The out-of-sample test of how ACIS performs on new
arms.

**Catch rate.** The share of injured pitchers ACIS flagged before their injured-list date. An
earlier framing (<Fact k="priorStudy.inSeason" /> in-season) now kept as internal history; the blind-cohort AUC is the
current headline.

**Fascial science.** The connective-tissue research base that runs parallel to ACIS. Treated as
"consistent with" ACIS's signals, never as a proven mechanism. See
[Biomechanics Roadmap](/biomechanics-roadmap).

**Fatigue meter.** How we describe ACIS. It measures accumulated arm fatigue, not a probability
of injury.

**Hawk-Eye.** MLB's optical tracking system and the source of the biomechanical data ACIS needs
for its next research horizon. Access is in legal review; no LOI today.

**Leave-season-out cross-validation.** The development test: train on every season but one, test
on the held-out season, rotate through. Prevents the model from looking good by memorizing a
single year.

**Research corpus.** The training and evaluation data: <Fact k="corpus.pitches" /> tracked pitches, <Fact k="corpus.appearances" />
pitcher-game appearances across 11 MLB seasons plus AAA. Distinct from the blind cohort; do not
fuse the two.

**SHAP.** The method that attributes each score to individual features, surfacing the top drivers
per read. Internal term only.

**Shear-wave elastography (SWE).** Ultrasound-based tissue-stiffness measurement, the direct
tissue read the Horizon 3 validation study would pair against ACIS scores.

**SP12.** The current scoring champion model. An XGBoost model. Internal name only.

**Sustained elevation.** The operative signal: the share of a pitcher's starts sitting at or
above the flag line, not whether they crossed it once. This is what separates the cohorts.

**Valid window.** The span where ACIS scores are trusted: from a pitcher's 5th start through the
first 65% of the season. Reads outside it are out of scope, not misses.

**Workload intelligence.** One of the approved descriptions of what ACIS provides. See
[GTM Rails](/gtm-rails).

**XGBoost.** The gradient-boosted decision-tree algorithm SP12 is built on. Internal term only.
