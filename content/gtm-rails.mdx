When in doubt, follow the lists here.

## Say this, not that

**Say:** fatigue monitoring, workload intelligence, arm health insights, durability
optimization, arm-fatigue index, post-game read.

**Never say:** injury prediction, injury prevention. {/* lint-language-disable-line: quotes the banned phrases to forbid them */} These two phrases are banned in every
material. The build itself will reject them.

**Also never say:**

- **Real-time or in-game.** ACIS is a post-game system. Full stop.
- **Data moat, proprietary data, or clean-room rebuild.** ACIS runs on public Statcast data.
  There is no defensible historical-data advantage, and claiming one is both false and
  fragile. The real edge is biomechanical data access plus distribution.
- **Anything mapping ACIS scores to fascial tissue states as proven.** Use "consistent with"
  and "designing research to test." See the [Biomechanics Roadmap](/biomechanics-roadmap).

## Keep the internals internal

The following are never named in outbound documents, decks, or conversations. They are fine
here on internal pages; they do not leave:

- The model name **<Fact k="internal.champion" />** and the fact that it is **XGBoost**
- **SHAP**, feature names, threshold values, and window lengths
- The term **AUC**. Outbound, express it in plain language: "it ranks the injured arm higher
  about four times out of five."

## Positioning in one breath

ACIS sees the arm-fatigue signal that precedes breakdown, a signal that is nearly invisible to
the box score, validated blind on arms it had never seen. The forward edge is biomechanical
data plus distribution, not a data moat.
